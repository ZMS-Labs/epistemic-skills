# Claim-evidence matrix

## Research question

For a single technically sophisticated homelab operator, does a pen-first visual AI canvas have enough plausible human-interface value, and does hybrid local/cloud inference have enough architectural value, to justify a reversible PenEcho pilot before ZMS commits to a deployment wrapper or fork?

## Capability negotiation

- Consensus: keyword search and mandatory record fetch were available and used.
- Scite: literature/full-text tool was present, but every attempted call returned the free-trial quota exhaustion message; the separate collections authentication canary was not exposed.
- Consequence: paper claims below are limited to fetched Consensus records. No Scite reception tallies, editorial notices, or full-text excerpts are claimed.

| Decision claim | Evidence | What it supports | What it does not support | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Handwriting may have a modest cognitive advantage over typing in some note-taking contexts. | Lau (2022), systematic review/meta-analysis, 33 reports/42 samples/88 effects, `g=0.144`. | Pen interaction is not a frivolous premise. | PenEcho efficacy, ideation gains, or a large effect. | Moderate |
| Stylus outcomes are conditional on user/task characteristics. | Bonneton-Botte et al. (2020), classroom field study; benefit depended on baseline level. | A personal device pilot is more informative than generic enthusiasm. | Adult homelab workflows. | Moderate-low |
| Digital spatial sketching can change concept generation behavior. | Yang & Lee (2020), VR-sketching pilot. | A visual canvas has a plausible mechanism beyond chat. | Generalization from VR/designers to PenEcho/operator. | Low-moderate |
| Creativity-support evidence is fragmented and prototype-heavy. | Frich et al. (2019), review of 143 HCI papers. | Product-specific testing is necessary; literature cannot settle adoption. | That PenEcho will fail. | Moderate |
| Edge inference can reduce data movement/latency exposure but creates communication/resource trade-offs. | Shi et al. (2020), survey. | Hybrid routing should be evaluated on measured workload. | That local is always faster, cheaper, or more private in practice. | Moderate |

## Coverage gaps

- No peer-reviewed PenEcho evaluation was found.
- No direct study of Supernote InkFlow with browser canvas applications was found.
- No paper establishes which local VLM will satisfy PenEcho's structured editable-draft contract.
- Scite reception and editorial checks were unavailable due quota, so the scholarly layer cannot be treated as fully validated.
